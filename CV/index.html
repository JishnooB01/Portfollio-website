About Me

Prajna Gangully is a dynamic individual from Chakdah, West Bengal, currently residing in Kolkata. She is a graduate with a Bachelor of Business Administration in Hospitality Management (BBAHM) and has gained practical experience through an internship as a receptionist. Beyond her academic and professional pursuits, Prajna is passionate about dance and is known for her love and care for stray dogs.

Her journey reflects a blend of academic achievement, professional experience, artistic expression, and compassion for animals, showcasing her multifaceted personality and commitment to making a positive impact in her community.

Prajna Ganguly’s dance journey began at the tender age of 3, when she first immersed herself in the world of Kathak. Over the years, her passion for dance expanded to include semi-classical and creative forms, becoming a powerful expression of her emotions. Dance has always been more than just a hobby for Prajna – it’s her true passion and a method of stress relief, allowing her to channel her energy and creativity. As a choreographer from Chakdah, she has crafted numerous performances, showcasing her talent and dedication. Prajna has graced many stages, performing in a variety of shows, and continues to evolve in her artistic journey. Her relentless pursuit of excellence has made her a respected figure in the dance community, inspiring others to embrace dance as both an art and a form of self-expression. Each performance is a testament to her love for the craft and her ongoing commitment to sharing the beauty of dance with the world.

Prajna Ganguly's academic journey has been marked by consistent dedication and a drive to excel. She completed her schooling at Satish Chandra Memorial School, graduating in 2018. She then pursued her higher secondary education through Rabindra Open Schooling, completing it in 2021. Continuing her educational pursuits, Prajna graduated with a Bachelor’s degree in Business Administration in Hospitality Management (BBAHM) from the Institute of Advanced Education and Research, passing out in 2024. During her time at the institute, she gained practical experience through internships as a receptionist and floor coordinator, further enhancing her skills in the hospitality industry. Prajna’s academic path reflects her commitment to both her professional growth and personal passions.
